Here is how this was derived:
  1. Set the price for standard 1 sqft 12x12” tile = $2.00
  2. Value – assumes that one is putting down with a “toothbrush” and therefore labor ratios with linear foot as does applied material.  This allows us to define the maximum value.  So a $2/sqft price becomes $24/sqft for a 1” tile that would have 12x the grout linear foot.
  3. Cost of additional material – To just cover the additional expected COLORSEAL costs.   In this case we are assuming a pint of Colorseal to cost $25/pint and coverage of 500 12x12 tile.
  4. Cost of additional material WITH 50% gross margin (so doubling the material cost).  If applying with machine the cost of labor does not change (only if using a toothbrush).  As such, the cost of Colorsealing small tiles becomes very profitable.

As less than 10% of the market is familiar with CLEANCARE Technology we do not want to give away the productivity savings associated with using them when we can (low lippage floors). This second look allows you to see the time impact using CLEANCARE will have. The third look is based on resin diamonds but allows you to enter a non-standard time based on your experience to estimate the ACTUAL TIME YOUR EXPECT your tech to require. This also provides the LOWEST Price you would be able to charge and still make a good margin. As such you will have your TARGET VALUE, your recommended scheduling time suitable for the youngest team member coming out of training and used to running resins at 5min, and your expected achievable times. The system will default your HONE AND POLISH estimate prices to the first case using the standard per section times of 5 min for marble, and 10 min for granite.
